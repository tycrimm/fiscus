A ·Absolute scale + hover legend

Restore the original split-y stacked area. A persistent side panel lists each band's current absolute value and share of gross. On hover the panel snaps to that day. Best for "what's the magnitude, what's the mix" without sacrificing precision.

B ·Dual panel — magnitude + composition

Two stacked charts share one x-axis. Top: absolute dollars (split-y, like A). Bottom: the same data normalized to 100% of gross. Synced crosshair. Reads as "totals on top, mix on bottom."

C ·Small multiples

Each component gets its own sparkline. Headline number on each card; share of gross + 30-day delta underneath. Best for spotting independent moves — a private mark-up can't hide inside a stack.

D ·One line per category (log y)

Six independent lines on a log y-axis so revolving (~$4k) and investments (~$1.8M) coexist legibly. Right-edge labels. Direct trajectory comparison; loses the at-a-glance sense of mix.

Net worth · per-category trajectory · log y

E ·Streamgraph (silhouette)

Centered streamgraph for asset bands; liability strip underneath. Total thickness = gross; band thickness = magnitude. Reads more as "shape of the portfolio over time" than as precise measurement.

Net worth · streamgraph

F ·Horizon chart

Each category as a single horizontal strip with three intensity bands encoding magnitude. Extremely compact — six categories in ~240px. Reads at a glance once you learn the encoding; rewards comparison.
